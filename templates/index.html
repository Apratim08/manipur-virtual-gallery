About Manipur's Artistic Heritage

Manipur, the "Jewel of India," represents a unique convergence of tribal and Vaishnavite traditions that have shaped its extraordinary artistic landscape. From the sacred villages of Andro and Thongjao where pottery is crafted without wheels, to the devotional performances of Rasa Lila under moonlit skies, every art form tells the story of spiritual devotion and cultural preservation.

Our gallery showcases five pillars of Manipuri heritage: the graceful Manipuri classical dance rooted in Bhakti traditions, the ancient pottery techniques of famous villages, exquisite handloom textiles, vibrant mythological paintings, and intricate handicrafts that connect generations. Each represents not just artistic mastery, but a living tradition that continues to thrive in modern Manipur.

Traditional Pottery of Manipur

Hand-Made Craftsmanship

  • Most pottery in Manipur is crafted without the potter's wheel
  • Artisans use a paddle and anvil technique or mold the clay entirely by hand
  • This technique gives the pottery a distinct, organic shape

Materials Used

  • Local clay, often mixed with powdered stone or sand
  • Serpentine stone clay known for its heat resistance

Communities Involved

  • Andro, Sekmai, Chairel, and Thongjao villages are famous for pottery
  • Women play a central role, especially among Purum and Tangkhul Naga tribes

Traditional Forms & Uses

  • Pots for cooking (chareng), storing water, fermenting rice beer
  • Figures of deities and ceremonial items
  • Black pottery and red pottery types based on firing process

Cultural Significance

  • Used in religious ceremonies and folk traditions
  • Created for festivals like Lai Haraoba
  • Essential for traditional healing rituals

Famous Pottery Villages

Andro Terracotta figures, ritual pots
Thongjao Red pottery for homes
Sekmai Fermentation pots for rice beer
Chairel Earthenware and cooking pots

Classical Manipuri Dance

Origin and Spiritual Roots

  • Originated in Manipur with rich tribal and Vaishnavite traditions
  • Strongly influenced by the Bhakti movement
  • Worship of Radha and Krishna is central
  • Performed during festivals like Janmashtami, Rath Yatra, and Lai Haraoba

Distinctive Features

  • Subtle facial expressions unlike dramatic forms
  • Flowing, circular movements resembling water and wind
  • Emphasis on devotion (bhakti rasa) over dramatic storytelling
  • Integral to temple rituals and community gatherings

Renowned Artists

  • Guru Bipin Singh - Renowned exponent and choreographer
  • Darshana Jhaveri - Prominent performer and promoter
  • Rajkumar Singhajit Singh - National award-winning dancer

Important Dance Forms

Rasa Lila Celebrates Krishna's love with Radha and gopis
Thang-Ta Martial art dance using sword and spear
Pung Cholom Dancers play pung drum with acrobatic moves
Kartal Cholom Dance using cymbals in Sankirtana performances
Nupa Pala Male devotional dance-singing form

Preservation and Promotion

Traditional pottery is under threat from modern utensils and mass-produced items. Efforts by NGOs and the Handicrafts Department of Manipur are helping revive and market these crafts. Pottery from Andro is showcased in cultural museums and heritage villages, keeping alive the ancient oral traditions and mythological storytelling of Manipur.
